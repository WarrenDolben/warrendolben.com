Bio

 I grew up on the Seacoast in Portsmouth, NH. I went to a small private university in South Western New York called Alfred University. I majored in English, and minored in Communications. I was also a four year member of the Saxons' swim team. I coached collegiate swimming as an Assistant Coach for several years; first at King's College in Wilkes-Barre, PA, then at Washington College in Chestertown, MD. I am enjoying learning the intricacies of new technologies, and feel that my attention to detail, and desire to hone my craft, will well serve clients' needs.
 I've been on this learning journey four the past three years now. I first started this site in 2017, and just the other day (1.26.21), I managed to make this site live on the web! It's amazing what a little persistence can do. Also, I'm exceedingly glad that I kept this via GitHub because this is a timeless gem. Ok, maybe I shouldn't go that far, but it's a nice reminder of how far I've come, and also how long I've been at it!
 It's truly amazing to look at this group of documents, and all the crazy antiquated ways I was doing things. For intstance, doing something weird with my tabbing (styling it in CSS), and also importing fonts in a longwinded fashion. It's no wonder that a recruiter once told me that my website looked "outdated," in reference to my work on my friend Nick's site. I've since made a better version, but have yet to send it over to him. Anyway, the point is I've come a long way since then, and I'm excited to keep experimenting with new technologies here, and continue to make this site better!
 My blog will be expiring at the end of the month, and I may be migrating content from there to here; which is probably what I will do eventually either way. Thanks for stopping by, and take a look before everything is updated and contemporary.
