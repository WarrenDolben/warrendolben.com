Tweaks, New Photos, Progress

10/20/24

 Welcome to my site! I've just made a few updates, and added some recent photos to the photo gallery the other day. I also changed the way the fonts are styled, switching to a very simple/less convoluted way of setting them, which made me feel good -- been looking at those too long. I figured I would post here, though I'm not sure how many readers I have(I suspect it is a very small number), and detail how things are going a bit.
 I've managed to get a few freelance jobs since I last posted, though nothing directly related to web development. The first one was doing voice recordings for an AI company overseas, and the second was somewhat related, I guess -- testing functionality of a website; a quick, and easy job. More recently I just did some research into companies with questionable data privacy histories/policies. Though I haven't been employed through web development, I have been working on my skills extensively most recently completing a test site for a would-be client and making it live on the internet. If you'd like to check it out you can go over to my dev playground where it is currently being hosted. It's a landing page for some sort of Crypto education company -- where individuals supposedly learn how to trade. Overall it's a little 'sus,' but it was good practice nonetheless. I'm excited to see what the future will bring.
 I've also taken on another venture -- another blog. It's a blog for people getting into web development, and I have a few posts on it now, though I haven't posted in a while. I do intend on posting about once per week at somepoint in the relatively near future, however. It can be cathartic, peaceful, and productive arranging ideas in your head and on the page in the form of a blog post, though it can be difficult to muster up 2500 words. I've started my next post, and will be putting in some more work on it shortly. Hopefully, I'll be completing a certification or two pretty soon, and I'll also be scoping out some freelance jobs per-usual. Thanks for stopping by.
